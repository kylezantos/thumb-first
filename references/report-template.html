Where the actions land

Hurff's zones describe the cost of a location, not where things must go. A "Delete" in the Ow zone is correct — the cost is the point. A frequent CTA there is the mistake. Each dot is one of Skillet's primary actions, plotted where it lives today.

Natural · low cost — effortless thumb arc. Primary actions, main nav, frequent controls.
Stretch · medium — reachable but the grip shifts. Secondary actions, settings.
Ow · high cost — a regrip to reach. Destructive or rare actions — or nothing important.
What's off

Two frequent actions — open menu (1) and add to pantry (2) — sit in the Ow zone. And Chat, the product's headline surface, has no dot at all: there's no visible affordance for it.

04

In the order I'd fix them

Ordered by user impact: critical (blocks or badly degrades the primary task) → important (real friction) → polish (refinement).

Critical2
01
CriticalRauschBudiu

Hamburger drawer is the wrong primary nav for this content

WhatFour stable destinations (My Pantry, Discover, My Tastes, About) plus Home plus Chat — the tab bar's natural range. Rausch is blunt: hamburger menus are "the tab bar's evil sibling." Tab bars measurably outperform them, and a first-time user opening Skillet sees zero destinations.

Why it mattersYour app's surface area is invisible. A returning user can find things; a new user has to discover by tapping a vague list icon top-left — high-cost reach, low semantic value.

Fix · decision neededReplace the drawer with a bottom tab bar (Home · Pantry · Discover · Chat · Profile — five, the sweet spot). Or, for a conversational-first identity, keep nav minimal but make Chat a visible persistent entry, not a side effect of typing. See the three options in §05.

02
CriticalRauschWroblewski

Chat — the actual product — has no visible navigation affordance

WhatThe only ways into Chat today are: type in the Home bar and send, get routed via a scan, or tap "Ask Skillet" inside Pantry's Add panel. There is no Chat tab, button, or "open conversation" affordance.

Why it mattersUsers who want to resume a conversation without composing a fresh message can't. It frames the product as a recipe app with a search bar, not an AI cooking assistant.

FixMake Chat a top-level destination. If you keep the conversational framing on Home, add at minimum an "Open chat" / "Past chats" affordance somewhere reachable — not buried in the history sheet inside chat.

Important4
03
ImportantHooberHurff

"Add to pantry" lives in the top-right Ow zone

WhatThe plus button on Pantry is top-right (40pt circle). Adding ingredients is a frequent, repeatable action for the core use case. Hurff would put frequent actions in the bottom Natural zone — and you've already established a bottom-action pattern on this same screen with "What can I make?"

Why it mattersThe secondary CTA gets the easy zone and the more frequent one gets the hard zone — that's backwards.

FixPull "Add" to a FAB above the bottom CTA, or merge the panel into a sheet that opens from a bottom action. The top-right plus is a desktop habit; the rest of the screen doesn't behave that way.

04
ImportantHurffde With

Persistent Ask Skillet bar on Home double-books the natural zone

WhatHome has both a sticky "Ask Skillet…" input and a "Make this recipe" CTA on the hero card. The input occupies the natural-zone real estate a tab bar would otherwise use, and pushes content up under a gradient mask.

Why it mattersIf finding 01 leads to a tab bar, the input and the tab bar will fight for the same band. Decide now — don't end up with both stacked.

Fix · silent forkA. Tab bar wins — the Ask Skillet entry collapses into a single Chat tab; Home is a content surface. B. Conversational entry wins — the input stays, no tab bar, Home and Chat are one surface. Both are valid; pick deliberately.

05
ImportantClarkAllen

Voice is hidden inside a text-input UI for a hands-busy product

WhatThe mic is a 30px circle inside the text input. For an app you'd use while your hands are covered in flour, voice is plausibly the primary input — not a tertiary affordance behind a text composer.

Why it mattersWhen hands are messy or full mid-prep, the only good input is voice. Treating it as a secondary inline glyph buries the only ergonomic answer.

FixConsider a voice-first composer — a prominent mic as default, text field on tap. At minimum, raise the mic to full size when typing isn't already in progress.

06
ImportantWroblewski

Pantry's "Ingredient name…" uses placeholder-as-label

WhatThe quick-add input has placeholder "Ingredient name…" with no persistent label — Wroblewski's most consistent rule on mobile forms.

Why it mattersThe placeholder vanishes exactly when the user starts typing — context gone at the moment of need.

FixTop-aligned visible label ("Ingredient name") with a format example below ("e.g. San Marzano tomatoes").

Polish2
07
PolishHooberPlatform Canon

Recipe back affordance is top-left only, mid-recipe

WhatWhile stepping through a curated recipe, the only way back is the top-left arrow.

Why it mattersA high-reach press to leave a screen you may flip in and out of while mixing. A redundant lower-zone exit would respect the use context.

FixA small "Exit recipe" / "Back to chat" link at the bottom of the recipe scroll, so leaving doesn't require a stretch.

08
PolishClark

Recipe step "tap to expand" has no visible affordance

WhatA collapsed step shows number + truncated title — tappable, but nothing signals it.

Why it mattersReturning to an earlier step requires the user to guess the collapsed row is interactive.

FixA chevron or "expand" glyph at the right edge of collapsed steps costs almost nothing and resolves it.

05

Primary navigation — pick one, deliberately

Findings 01, 02 and 04 are entangled — they want one design pass, not three patches. Three coherent answers, side by side.

Option A · current

Hamburger drawer

Top-left list icon opens a side sheet with four destinations. Home owns the conversation entry.
  • CONHides all destinations behind one ambiguous icon.
  • CONHigh-cost top-left reach for primary nav.
  • CONChat — your product — has no entry of its own.
  • PROMaximum content space on Home.
Option B · recommended

Bottom tab bar

Five destinations (Home · Pantry · Chat · Discover · Profile) in the natural zone. Conversation becomes a real surface.
  • PROEvery destination visible at first launch.
  • PROChat becomes a first-class surface.
  • PROAll nav lives in the natural thumb zone.
  • CONAsk Skillet bar must go (or live inside the Chat tab only).
Option C · alternative

Conversational shell

Embrace the Ask-Skillet bar as the product's spine. Home and Chat are one surface — no tabs, no drawer.
  • PROStrongest identity — Skillet is conversation, not chrome.
  • PROVoice and text live in one prominent surface (resolves 05).
  • CONPantry / Discover / Profile must surface inside conversation.
  • CONHigher discovery cost for non-conversational features.
06

Decisions that were made silently

Not necessarily wrong — but worth surfacing as decisions, not defaults.

Where Chat lives in the mental model

Side effect of composition vs. first-class destination. Currently a side effect. See 02 and 05.

Input mode hierarchy

Text-first with mic vs. voice-first with text fallback. Currently text-first; the hands-busy use case leans voice.

Add-to-Pantry surfacing

Top-right plus vs. bottom CTA vs. scan-first. The plus was a desktop default; nothing on the screen earned it.

Recipe "back step" semantics

Visible chevron vs. discoverable-by-tap. Currently the second. Cheap to upgrade.
